Ador Fellowship 2024 – My Experience

In 2024, I was selected for the Ador Fellowship, a fully funded program by Maker's Asylum in Goa. Only 8 students are selected, which made it a very special achievement for me.

I discovered this opportunity through Ashish Shende who is the lab manager of Nalanda Abhiyan Lab and also worked in Makers Asylum while learning CAD design and electronics. The application process included a form and a short video where I had to express my ideas.

In the next stage, I identified a real-world problem—improper disposal of sanitary pads, which causes pollution and health risks. To solve this, I designed EcoBurn, a compact incinerator using Arduino, a heating coil, relay, and switches. I also created a 3D model for the concept.

After getting selected, we had an initial meeting and then our classes started. Every weekend, we attended online sessions where we learned CAD, electronics, IoT, and PCB design.

Since I already had experience with Fusion 360, I was comfortable with CAD, but I still learned many new things through tasks and challenges. The electronics sessions also helped me understand real-world systems better.

The total learning duration was around 60 hours. This experience helped me improve my technical skills, problem-solving ability, and confidence to work on real-world ideas.
